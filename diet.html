Nutrition & Diets

Find the fuel that works for your body. Whether for weight loss or muscle gain, consistency is key.

Eating for Muscle Gain

To build muscle, you need proper food to help you along your journey. The main thing you should be looking for is something protein heavy.

Top Recommended Foods:
  • Chicken and Rice: A popular meal that assists in muscle gain.
  • Lean Proteins: Eggs, tuna, turkey, salmon, lean beef, shrimp.
  • Dairy & Others: Greek yogurt, cottage cheese, beans, avocado.

Mediterranean

Based on countries bordering the Mediterranean Sea. Includes fruits, vegetables, fish, legumes, and olive oil.

  • Low caloric density, high nutrients.
  • Promotes fruits as dessert; cuts out red meats.

Pescatarian

Simple to manage and good for weight loss. Cuts out red meat and poultry, making fish the only meat.

  • Rich in fiber and protein to make you feel full.
  • Requires cutting out sugary snacks/treats.

Intermittent Fasting

A diet that limits when you eat rather than what you eat.

  • 5:2 Method: Eat normally 5 days, low calorie for 2 days.
  • Time Restricted: Fasting for 16-18 hours a day (eating window of 6-8 hours).

Paleo

Revolves around eating unprocessed foods: meat, fish, eggs, vegetables, fruits, seeds, and nuts.

  • Cuts out all processed foods.
  • High protein and fiber can be very filling.

Animal-Based

Consists of meat, honey, dairy, and eggs. It is the best bioavailable source of essential nutrients.

  • Nutrient Dense: Rich in B12, Iron, and Zinc to support immune and neurological function.
  • Muscle Health: Complete proteins help preserve muscle and sustain satiety.

Volumetrics

Focuses on food dense with nutrients and low in calories.

  • Category 1 (Unlimited): Non-starchy veggies, broth soups.
  • Category 4 (Avoid): Chips, fried foods, candy.
