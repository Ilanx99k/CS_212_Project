Workout Splits Explained

Choosing your split depends on your time, dedication, and goals. Muscle groups must be targeted twice a week for growth.

*For health, just an hour a week of resistance training can lower the risk of death by 30%.

Full Body (2 Days/Week)

Goal: Least time commitment. Hitting every single muscle is difficult.

Focus: Priority on compound movements.

Key Exercises:

  • Squat variant (back/hack/front)
  • Hinge variant (Romanian/Traditional Deadlift)
  • Horizontal push (flat/incline bench)
  • Vertical push (overhead press)
  • Vertical pull (lat pulldowns/pullups)
  • Horizontal push (barbell/machine/cable row)

Upper/Lower (4 Days/Week)

Goal: Best blend of efficiency and intensity for intermediate lifters.

Structure: Upper body and Lower body done twice weekly.

Upper Body Days:

  • Pressing, pulling, and arm work.
  • Bench press, Overhead press, Rows, Pull-ups.

Lower Body Days:

  • Compound leg movements and posterior chain.
  • Squats, Deadlifts, Leg press, Curls.

Push/Pull/Legs (6 Days/Week)

Goal: High-frequency, high-volume approach for hypertrophy.

Note: Requires good sleep, adequate calories, and consistent discipline.

Structure: Push, Pull, Legs cycled 2 times in a week. Typically 1 cycle then a rest day into the next cycle.

  • Push: Chest, Shoulders, Triceps (Bench, Overhead Press, Dips).
  • Pull: Back, Rear Delts, Biceps (Rows, Pullups/Pulldowns, Curls).
  • Legs: Quads, Hamstrings, Glutes (Squats, RDLs, Leg Press).
