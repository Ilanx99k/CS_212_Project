Workout Splits Explained

Choosing your split depends on your time, dedication, and goals. Remember, to maximize growth, muscle groups must be targeted twice a week.

*For general health, just an hour a week of resistance training can lower the risk of death by 30%.

Full Body (2 Days/Week)

Goal:

Least time commitment / General Training. Hitting every single muscle would be difficult.

Focus:

Priority on compound movements for maximum impact from each exercise.

Example Exercises:

  • Squat variant (back/hack/front)
  • Hinge variant (Romanian/Traditional Deadlift)
  • Horizontal push (flat/incline bench, dumbbells/barbell)
  • Vertical push (overhead press barbell or dumbbell)
  • Vertical pull (lat pulldowns/pullups)
  • Horizontal pull (barbell/machine/cable row)

Upper/Lower (4 Days/Week)

Goal:

Balance between frequency and recovery. Supports progressive overload very well.

Best For:

Intermediate liftersâ€”best blend of efficiency and intensity.

Upper Body Focus:

  • Horizontal press (bench press or dumbbell bench)
  • Vertical press (overhead press)
  • Horizontal pull (barbell or cable row)
  • Vertical pull (lat pulldown or pull-up)
  • Chest isolation, Shoulder isolation, Arm work

Lower Body Focus:

  • Squat and Hinge variants
  • Quad accessory (leg press, lunges, or step-ups)
  • Hamstring accessory (leg curls)
  • Glute emphasis (hip thrusts or cable kickbacks)
  • Calves (standing or seated raises)

Push/Pull/Legs (6 Days/Week)

Goal:

High-frequency, high-volume training for excellent hypertrophy (muscle growth).

Best For:

Most demanding routine. Requires good sleep, adequate calories, and consistent discipline.

Structure:

  • Push (Chest, Shoulders, Triceps): Includes horizontal/incline press, overhead press, and triceps isolation.
  • Pull (Back, Rear Delts, Biceps): Includes horizontal/vertical pulls, rear delt isolation, and various biceps curls.
  • Legs (Quads, Hamstrings, Glutes, Calves): Includes squat/hinge variants, and specific accessories for quads, hams, glutes, and calves.
